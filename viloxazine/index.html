Viloxazine

Jul 20, 2021
experience report

Recently I posted a picture of a “connoisseur kit” featuring this re-branded Viloxazine formulation, the new Irresponsible Mother’s Little Helper, “Totally Not Speed” for belligerent children who won’t stay seated in their damn chairs, or whatever.

Viloxazine is an old antidepressant with a complex pharmacology (NRI / 5-H2B antag / 5-HT2C ag / 5-HT7 antag).

The posted picture garnered some attention, specifically from a handful of my ADHD co-diagnosees, who started screaming at me about the benefits of medicating very young children. So, I hope my vow to take a high dose of that shit, with calculated disregard for the “extended-release” mechanism, did not go unnoticed.

(~300 mg, crushed extended release pellets, oral) I confidently expected to be uncomfortably restless and dysphoric. The effect arc was far gentler and more agreeable than that of any drug sitting in the same pharmacological wheelhouse as stimulants might promise to be. Locked in a whole-body hum or buzz, like coming up on a low dose of Psilocybin, I remember wanting to take deeper breaths, because the act breathing was quite pleasurable and even anxiolytic. Socializing on this will likely seem less burdensome for the introverted folks out there.

If there was a word for a gratifying and desirable equivalent of akathisia, it would be printed on the drug’s package insert. To reach for something more quasiromantic, think of the emotional itchiness that is felt in anticipation of inevitable infatuation, when a mild obsession with an attractive individual that checks all the checkable boxes is coming over the hill and you’re helpless in the most welcome way. If this is not a familiar feeling to you, but you’ve tried 2C-D, then Viloxazine is 2C-D Ultra Lite, with all of its nootropic repertoire, minus the emotional instability. And it surely isn’t some blank-slate tofu — it’s very much a standalone main course.
